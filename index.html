Etymology

From draco- + griff, from Ancient Greek δράκων (drákōn, "dragon") + γρύψ (grúps, "griffin").

Noun

dracogriff (she/they)

  1. Any one of the numerous species belonging to several genera within the family Gryphus.

Description

Dracogriff (Gryphus dracogryphus), also known as Draco, is a Korean-Canadian artist, worldbuilder, and photographer in her early 20s. She is a hard sci-fi and speculative biology enthusiast.

From beak to tailtip, Dracogriff measures ℓ m, almost half of which is tail. Mass is approximately ██ kg. Wing chord is ██ cm, with wingspan of ███ cm. The presence of secondary eyes is notable, as is the presence of highly dextrous wing digits. Plumage is white, with iridescent feathers and bioluminescent sky-blue highlights.

The most usual call is a short cawcaw! or a soft and melodic mrow. Dracogriff can also produce a wide variety of sounds and sometimes mimic noises made by other animals. She has demonstrated a particular affinity for mimicking human speech.

Dracogriff has been noted to be intelligent. She has a brain-to-body ratio equivalent to that of a human, which has led to some to speculate that she is self-aware and potentially sophont.[citation needed]
